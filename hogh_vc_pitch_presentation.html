Patent strategy (four families)

NegotiationCoach™ — dual-sided offer intelligence

At offer time, we give the employer team and the candidate parallel, private coaching in real time. Agents do not negotiate with each other and cannot originate terms; humans remain the only parties who can move the deal. That design matches how trust and liability actually work in comp conversations.

Why it’s defensible: The architecture targets simultaneous asymmetric advisory during live employment negotiations—meaningfully different from a single-sided copilot or an ATS email assistant.

Candidate Intelligence Brief™ — synthesis, not search results

We combine heterogeneous public and structured signals into one recruiter-facing narrative: motivations, risks, and conversation guidance—not a pile of tabs and links. That is where time savings and quality gains show up in the workflow.

Why it’s defensible: Claims can cover the structured brief format, motivation mapping, and provenance-aware synthesis—not generic “LLM summarizes the internet.”

Talent Intelligence Graph — compounding memory

People, skills, roles, and organizations live in a graph that tightens with hiring outcomes over time, including trajectory-style reasoning rather than a frozen taxonomy. More enterprise usage makes the model of the labor market sharper, not just more data in a warehouse.

Why it’s defensible: Outcome-linked graph updates and GNN-style inference in this domain are a specific technical story—not “we installed Neo4j.”

Fairness-aware orchestration — built into the agent stack

Fairness constraints sit at agent decision gates: when a threshold is breached, the system can stop, escalate to a person, attach SHAP-style attribution to the suspect signal, and suggest remediation. That is how you pass a serious HR, legal, or regulator conversation.

Why it’s defensible: Fairness is part of the multi-agent control plane, not a one-off compliance report layered onto a black box.

Research roadmap (credibility + hiring)

We intend to publish in top ML and HCI venues (NeurIPS, ICML, CHI, EMNLP) so our methods are peer-reviewed where it counts. Each stream ties a product bet to a clear technical claim:

  • Human offer negotiations with dual-sided advisory AI Working title: NegotiationCoach: Real-Time Dual-Sided Advisory AI for Human Employment Offer Negotiations — frames how private, real-time guidance coexists with human-only deal-making.
  • Multi-source evidence → recruiter-grade briefs Working title: Beyond the Resume: Multi-Source Candidate Signal Synthesis using Large Language Models for Human Recruiter Augmentation — makes the synthesis problem explicit, not “better search.”
  • Fairness constraints across multi-agent hiring pipelines Working title: Fair by Design: Embedding Demographic Parity in Multi-Agent Recruiting Pipelines — connects governance to architecture, not slide-deck ethics.
  • Career trajectories on heterogeneous graphs Working title: Career Trajectory Prediction with Heterogeneous Graph Neural Networks — supports the graph as a learning system, not a static ontology.
