If "The salary band is fixed — we can't go higher."
Don't accept or withdraw. This is a standard opening position, not a final answer. Pivot immediately to the equity cliff.

"I completely understand — I know bands are set centrally. I'd love to make this work. Could we look at reducing the vesting cliff from 4 years to 3? And separately, is there any flexibility on a one-time sign-on to bridge the gap between the offer and my current package?"
If "We can go to €90K, but that's our max."
€90K is a good result but not your target. Accept the base increment and immediately shift to sign-on.

"€90K works — thank you for moving on that. Given I'm coming in slightly below my current total comp, would you consider a one-time sign-on of €4K? It doesn't affect the ongoing salary structure and would really help me make the transition work."
If "Another candidate has accepted at this level."
This is a pressure tactic — don't react emotionally. Stay grounded and keep the conversation factual.

"I appreciate the transparency — I'd still like to make this work. I'm specifically looking at the gap between the offer and the market median for this role in the European market, which sits around €90K. Is there any flexibility there, or on the vesting terms? I want to say yes."
If "We need a decision by end of day."
Don't be pressured into deciding without your final negotiation attempt completed. Ask for 24 hours — this is always reasonable.

"I want to make the right decision and I want to join TechCorp. Could I have until tomorrow noon? I just want to make sure the family-side logistics work — and I'd love to have one more quick conversation about the vesting terms before I sign."
