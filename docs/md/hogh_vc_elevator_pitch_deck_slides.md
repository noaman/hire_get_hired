## Slide 2 — The Problem (Dual-Sided Crisis)

### 1. Objective
Prove the problem is **large, expensive, and structurally broken** on **both** sides.

### 2. Context and message
Employers suffer **slow fills**, **high cost-per-hire**, and **recruiter time** sunk in logistics. Candidates suffer **opacity**, **low response**, and **negotiation disadvantage**. Incumbent stacks are **siloed** (sourcing vs ATS vs scheduling)—no unified autonomous layer from requisition to offer.

### 3. Data / facts to use
- **42 days** average time-to-fill; **$4,700+** direct recruiting cost per hire (role-dependent up to **~$28K** all-in).
- **~60%+** recruiter time on repetitive tasks (sourcing, scheduling, admin).
- **73%** of job seekers report poor/opaque application experience.
- **2–4%** application response rates; **~5.1 months** average search duration.
- **57%** accept first offer; **$5K–$15K** commonly left on table.
- **78%** ATS adoption but **94%** of candidate profiles never re-surfaced.
- Agentic HR AI market **~$2.5B (2023)** → **~$23.2B by 2034**, **~35% CAGR** (research—cite as analyst-sourced in deck footnotes).

### 4. What this slide is about (lasting message)
This is a **systems problem**, not a UI problem: data exists, but **no one synthesizes it end-to-end** for **both** recruiter and candidate. The takeaway: **huge pain + clear waste** → room for a **new architectural layer**, not another point tool.

## Slide 3 — The Solution (HOGH in One Breath)

### 1. Objective
Show **what you built** and **how it changes outcomes** in plain language.

### 2. Context and message
**TalentAgent™** automates research, logistics, and synthesis; delivers **Candidate Intelligence Brief™** before interviews. **CareerAgent™** runs continuous opportunity intelligence, applications (with approval), interview prep, and offer coaching; delivers **Opportunity Intelligence Brief™** before interviews. **NegotiationCoach™** gives **private, real-time** guidance during **human-to-human** offer talks. **Human Handoff Protocol™** defines where AI stops and humans decide.

### 3. Data / facts to use
- **Human-centered pipeline:** intelligence gathering → match/outreach (with approvals) → pre-interview briefs → **human interview** → offer with **AI advisory** → post-hire retention.
- Pilot-style industry benchmarks (research): **30–45%** time-to-fill reduction; **2×** outreach response vs templates; **40%** scheduling friction reduction; **+15 NPS** for scheduling bots—use as **category validation**, not yet HOGH-specific claims.

### 4. What this slide is about (lasting message)
HOGH is **opposite** “AI replaces recruiters/candidates.” The product is **preparation at scale** + **compliance-friendly gates**. Audience remembers: **two agents, two briefs, humans in the room for the decisions that matter.**

## Slide 4 — Why Now (Market & Adoption Tailwinds)

### 1. Objective
Create **urgency**: technology + regulation + buyer behavior align for **agentic orchestration**.

### 2. Context and message
Enterprises are moving from **generative copilots** to **multi-agent workflows**. Capital is flowing to agentic HR. Regulation (GDPR, CCPA, **EU AI Act** high-risk hiring) **rewards** documented human oversight—not black-box automation.

### 3. Data / facts to use
- **~38%** of Fortune 500 HR departments with **≥1 agentic recruiting module by Q3 2025** vs **7% in 2022** (research).
- VC: **~$1.9B** into agentic HR AI **2022–2025** (research).
- **EU AI Act:** recruitment as **high-risk** → **human-in-the-loop** (aligns product design).

### 4. What this slide is about (lasting message)
**Window is open**: buyers are piloting agents, budgets exist, and **governance-first** vendors win enterprise. HOGH isn’t an early science project—it’s **timely infrastructure** for how hiring **must** work under modern AI law and board risk oversight.
